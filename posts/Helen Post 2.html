I am a student at the University of Pennsylvania looking for affordable food options around the University of Pennsylvania. Give me various food options at difference price points and different cuisine.

Here are five varied food-spots at different price points and cuisines, all within walking distance of University of Pennsylvania (Philadelphia, PA) — great for a student budget with some variety! I’ve grouped them roughly from lowest to slightly higher price.

1. Budget / Grab-&-go

Bui’s Lunch Truck

Bui’s Lunch Truck Web Address: 298 S University Ave, Philadelphia, PA 19104 Phone: +18566307269

  • Location: 298 S University Ave, Philadelphia, PA 19104.
  • Price point: “$1-10” according to listing — the most budget friendly option here.
  • Why it’s good: Perfect for a quick bite between classes; sandwiches/single items without a big time commitment.
  • Tip: Arrive earlier for better selection (likely crowds when lunch rush hits).

2. Casual & Affordable International

Cucina Zapata

Cucina Zapata Address: 3101 Ludlow St, Philadelphia, PA 19104

  • Location: 3101 Ludlow St, Philadelphia, PA 19104.
  • Price: Listed $10-20.
  • Cuisine: Mexican / street-food style; a good step up from super budget while still student-friendly.
  • Why it stands out: A fun change from standard campus food; good for lunch or a lighter dinner.
  • Tip: Maybe share a dish or two if you’re on a tighter budget.

3. Ethnic Sit-down for Under ~$15-20

Abyssinia (Ethiopian)

Abyssinia Web Address: 229 S 45th St, Philadelphia, PA 19104 Phone: +12153872424

  • Location: 229 S 45th St, Philadelphia, PA 19104.
  • Price: $10-20.
  • Cuisine: Ethiopian — communal, flavorful, and a nice change of pace.
  • Why it works: Offers a solid sit-down experience without breaking the bank.
  • Tip: Try sharing a platter with a friend so you can sample more and split cost.

4. Classic Student-Friendly Mediterranean

Greek Lady

Greek Lady Web Address: 222 S 40th St, Philadelphia, PA 19104 Phone: +12153822600

  • Location: 222 S 40th St, Philadelphia, PA 19104.
  • Price: $10-20 range.
  • Cuisine: Greek / Mediterranean.
  • Why it’s good: Reliable, pleasant setting for lunch or dinner; easy to access.
  • Tip: Choose a gyro or souvlaki plate for good value; ask for extra veggies if you want a lighter option.

5. Faster‐Casual Chicken / Late Night Option

Nanu’s Hot Chicken

Nanu’s Hot Chicken Web Address: 3301 Market St, Philadelphia, PA 19104 Phone: +12159188838

  • Location: 3301 Market St, Philadelphia, PA 19104.
  • Price: $10-20.
  • Cuisine: Hot chicken / hearty comfort food; good for a more relaxed dinner or post-class meal.
  • Why it works: Slightly indulgent but still in student budget; good if you want something less standard.
  • Tip: If spice isn’t your thing, ask for their milder level; great place to go with friends.

Final thoughts & budgeting tips:

  • If you’re keeping costs low, try to aim for ≤ $10-12 for lunch. Bui’s Lunch Truck hits that.
  • For dinners or a slightly fancier treat, the $10-20 places above still keep you under many restaurant averages.
  • Alternate cuisines help keep things interesting (Mexican, Ethiopian, Greek, chicken comfort).
  • Consider sharing dishes or doing lunch instead of dinner when feasible — lunch menus tend to be cheaper.
  • Use off-peak hours (before 6 pm) for less wait and maybe better deals.
  • Don’t forget: campus dining halls / food courts (e.g., “Shops at Penn” options) can also offer very affordable meals. (shopsatpenn.com)

Here are 10 affordable food spots near University of Pennsylvania (Philadelphia, PA), covering different cuisines and price-points so you can mix up your meals without overspending. I’ve grouped them roughly into ≤ $10, $10-20, and $20+ (for times when you want a little treat).

≤ $10 / Very Budget-Friendly

1. Nafi Food Express

Nafi Food Express Address: 3400 Market St, Philadelphia, PA 19104 Phone: +12673345830

  • Cuisine: Halal / Indian street-food style with items under ~$8. (The Infatuation)
  • Why go: Great value if you’re on a tight budget and want something flavorful.
  • Tip: Go for the grilled lamb or vegetable biryani for a satisfying meal.

2. Food Trucks & Cart Options (general)

  • These aren’t specific restaurants but several food-carts near campus are known for super low-cost meals (often ≤ $10) and quick service. (The Phoenix)
  • Why: Ideal for grab-and-go between classes.
  • Tip: Arrive before peak lunch rush (~12:00-12:30pm) to avoid long lines.

$10-20 / Good Student Range

3. Dig Inn

  • Address: 140 S 36th St, Philadelphia, PA 19104.
  • Cuisine: Healthy-casual bowls and veggies with meats/plant-based options.
  • Why: Slight step up from ultra-budget, still very student-friendly.
  • Tip: Choose a bowl with base+two sides to get full value.

4. Sabrina’s Café

Sabrina’s Cafe Web Address: 1804 Callowhill St, Philadelphia, PA 19130 Phone: +12156369061

  • Address: 227 N 34th St, Philadelphia, PA 19104.
  • Cuisine: American brunch/comfort; good for a mid-range meal (~$10-20).
  • Why: Fun spot for a relaxed meal or weekend brunch.
  • Tip: If going brunch time, arrive early to avoid wait.

5. Shake Shack – University City

  • Address: 3200 Chestnut St, Philadelphia, PA 19104.
  • Cuisine: American burgers/fast-casual; still within student budget.
  • Why: Reliable, quick-service option when you want a simpler meal.
  • Tip: Share a combo if you’re not extra hungry to save a few dollars.

6. Saad’s Halal Restaurant

  • Address: 4500 Walnut St, Philadelphia, PA 19139.
  • Cuisine: Middle Eastern / Halal; good value.
  • Why: Big portions, modest pricing, convenient to campus.
  • Tip: Try the chicken over rice or kebab plate if you want a hearty meal.

$20+ / Treat Yourself / Sit-Down

7. White Dog Café – University City

  • Address: 3420 Sansom St, Philadelphia, PA 19104.
  • Cuisine: Contemporary American, locally-sourced ingredients.
  • Why: A step-up for when you want a nicer meal — still student friendly if you pick wisely (shared appetizer + main).
  • Tip: Check for lunch specials or daytime deals for better value.

8. Han Dynasty University City

  • Address: 3711 Market St, Philadelphia, PA 19104.
  • Cuisine: Sichuan-style Chinese, a bit more upscale in taste and price (~$20+ for full courses).
  • Why: Great when you want something more adventurous and are dining with friends.
  • Tip: Share dishes family-style to keep cost per person down.

9. CO‑OP Restaurant & Bar

  • Address: 20 S 33rd St, Philadelphia, PA 19104.
  • Cuisine: American bistro-style with bar.
  • Why: Good for evenings or meeting up for dinner + drinks; a “nice” option within reach.
  • Tip: Consider going for just apps + shared entree if you want to keep budget moderate.

10. Bonus – A bakery/café pick

  • Not a full sit-down restaurant, but worth noting: the bake shop at Walnut Hill College (near campus) sells student-friendly pastries & sandwiches at very reasonable prices. (Reddit)
  • Why: Great for breakfast or light lunch on a budget.
  • Tip: Grab a sandwich + coffee and sit on campus or in a nearby park.

Final Tips for Student Dining & Budgeting

  • Try eating lunch instead of dinner when possible — many places have better value midday.
  • Share dishes with a friend: especially at the $20+ places, splitting mains or desserts helps.
  • Use off-peak times (before 6 pm) to avoid crowds and get better seating.
  • Ask about student discounts or early-bird specials; some places offer them.
  • Explore the many food-carts around campus for ultra-budget meals (<$10).
  • Keep variety in mind: mix cuisines, price-points, and settings so you don’t get food fatigue.

Reflection

From my point of view, the list of food options around Penn is actually really helpful because it reflects the range of what I usually look for — quick, affordable bites between classes, but also a few nicer sit-down places when I want to treat myself or meet friends. I appreciate how it covers different cuisines — from Ethiopian to Mexican to Halal — since eating the same thing every day can get dull fast. The price breakdowns also make it easier to plan around my budget, especially since being a student means constantly balancing convenience with cost. I like that some places, like Bui’s Lunch Truck and Nafi Food Express, are under $10, while others like White Dog Café or Han Dynasty are there for when I want a more relaxed dinner. It gives a nice sense of variety without being overwhelming. That said, I could’ve asked for a few extra things to make the list even more personalized. For example, it might have helped to include more information about hours — since late-night or early-morning options are often what students really need — and maybe a section specifically for vegetarian or healthier choices on a budget. I also could’ve asked for options that are open during weekends or offer good study environments, since I sometimes want a café-style space to read or work. Another angle would’ve been getting suggestions for delivery or campus dining alternatives, especially those that take PennCash or student discounts. Overall, the list is a strong start, but with a few tweaks focused on schedule, diet, and study needs, it could become a perfect student dining guide.
