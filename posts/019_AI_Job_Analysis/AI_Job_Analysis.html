Prompt

How do you think the future of the job market will look as a result of AI. What jobs do you think might be more at risk? Do you have evidence for that? How do you think the government might handle the higher unemployment?

Alright — here are all three: (A) most at-risk jobs, (B) jobs AI will increase demand for, and (C) a tailored analysis for your world (finance + consulting + political science + law-adjacent IR work). I’ll keep it super clear and grounded in real evidence/trends.

A) The 20 Jobs Most at Risk From AI (Ranked + Why)

(Based on IMF/OECD exposure studies, WEF task-automation data, and company-level hiring freezes.)

1. Data entry clerks

Fully automatable — LLMs handle structured text perfectly.

2. Basic administrative assistants

Scheduling, email filtering, drafting memos = trivial for AI.

3. Customer support & call centers

Already being replaced by AI agents and chatbots.

4. Paralegals & legal researchers

AI performs case summarization, contract review, discovery.

5. Junior financial analysts

AI does modeling templates, comps, memos, and dashboards.

6. Accounts payable/receivable clerks

Invoice processing & reconciliation are easily automated.

7. Payroll clerks & HR screeners

AI can screen resumes, manage onboarding, handle compliance docs.

8. Basic copywriters & content writers

SEO writing, product descriptions, social media drafts.

9. Market researchers (entry-level)

AI does survey analysis, segmentation, data summaries.

10. Journalists (low-tier / “content mills”)

Local news + aggregation especially vulnerable.

11. Insurance underwriters

Algorithmic risk scoring replaces heavy routine work.

12. Real estate assistants

AI handles listings, comps, outreach, client qualification.

13. Technical support (Tier 1)

AI solves common troubleshooting faster.

14. Proofreaders

Grammar tools are already extremely strong.

15. Loan officers (initial screening)

Automated credit scoring + income verification.

16. Translators (non-literary)

Straightforward translation is nearly solved.

17. Bookkeepers

Accounting software + AI categorization.

18. Executive assistants (traditional)

Travel booking, note-taking, admin ops largely automatable.

19. Basic graphic designers

Logos, mockups, simple visuals — AI does them instantly.

20. Telemarketers & sales development reps

LLM-based outreach tools are already replacing SDR roles.

Pattern: These jobs share 3 traits:

  • repetitive tasks
  • rule-based reasoning
  • heavy text or structured data

B) Jobs AI Will Increase Demand For

1. AI workflow designers (“prompt engineers”)

People who build automation chains for businesses. Demand exploding.

2. Data scientists & ML engineers

AI multiplies their reach — but humans still architect systems.

3. AI product managers

Massive hiring across tech + finance + enterprise SaaS.

4. Cybersecurity analysts

AI increases attack surfaces; demand for defenders skyrockets.

5. Automation integration managers

People who connect AI tools to real operations (CRM, ERP, etc.)

6. Compliance, audit, & model-risk managers

Regulators now expect AI governance — huge growth area (your world of bank auditing fits here).

7. Human-centered fields

  • nursing
  • therapy/counseling
  • social work
  • teaching These require emotional labor AI can’t replicate.

8. Skilled trades

Electricians, plumbers, mechanics. AI doesn’t have hands.

9. High-level creative direction

AI handles drafts; humans direct strategy, taste, and final judgment.

10. Diplomacy, negotiation, lawmaking, and IR analysis

AI can support research but can’t build coalitions or wield political legitimacy.

C) PERSONALIZED ANALYSIS — YOUR FIELDS (finance + consulting + IR/law + politics)

You’re in a really interesting hybrid of fields — and AI affects each differently.

1. Investment Banking / Private Equity / Consulting

Tasks heavily hit:

  • drafting CIMs, teasers, board decks
  • building vanilla financial models
  • comps / precedent searches
  • due diligence summaries
  • industry overviews
  • client-ready writeups

Large banks (including BofA) are already piloting AI tools that do 60–70% of this.

But here’s the key:

AI is replacing tasks, not bankers. Humans are still needed for:

  • negotiations
  • client relationship management
  • judgment calls on risk
  • managing deals
  • handling politics
  • persuasion & trust

Real risk: fewer junior seats. Not zero — but fewer.

Your positioning:

You’re actually well-positioned because:

  • you understand both finance AND political/regulatory environments
  • you understand high-level judgment (IR theory, geopolitics)
  • you have communication skills that AI can’t replicate
  • you’re strong at synthesis and persuasion

Those are the EXACT skills AI cannot substitute.

2. Government, International Relations, Policy, Law

These are not highly automatable.

AI can assist in:

  • drafting briefs
  • summarizing laws
  • translating documents
  • analyzing huge datasets

But cannot replace:

  • political legitimacy
  • coalition-building
  • human negotiation
  • strategic persuasion
  • legal advocacy
  • public accountability
  • in-person diplomacy

IR is fundamentally about humans, power, and perception — AI is just a tool.

Your interest in political science, trade law, and populism is inherently “human conflict + institutions,” which AI cannot mediate alone.

3. Academic analysis, critical theory, philosophy

These fields don’t automate well because:

  • new ideas require originality + worldview
  • scholarship requires judgment
  • writing has a voice and identity AI helps, but cannot be the scholar.

You’re safe here too.

BOTTOM LINE FOR YOU

Your risk zones:

  • repetitive financial modeling
  • basic memo drafting
  • low-level research
  • entry-stage analyst work

Your advantage zones:

  • IR + finance + law hybrid expertise
  • geopolitical analysis
  • persuasion, strategy, rhetoric
  • leadership & communication
  • regulatory interpretation
  • complex judgment under uncertainty
  • relationship-driven roles (IB, consulting, diplomacy, public sector)
  • anything requiring political sensitivity or human trust

People like you — who blend economics, law, geopolitics, analysis, writing, and interpersonal intelligence — tend to survive AND benefit from AI transitions.
