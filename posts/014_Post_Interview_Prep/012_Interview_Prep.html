Prompt

I have a group placement interview for my internship next summer. I am being interviewed by M&A, Consumer and Retail, and TMT groups. Some very common questions I could be asked include, “why this group?” “what are some trends in our industry you see,” “what is a deal BofA has advised on recently in this industry?” What are reccomendations you have for me?

Excellent — this is a critical stage in the internship process, and the group placement interview is where you show that you’ve gone beyond generic banking prep and have real interest and knowledge about each coverage vertical. Here’s a structured plan with targeted recommendations:

1. Structure Your “Why This Group?” Answers

Each answer should have three parts:

  1. Personal motivation (why you’re drawn to the industry or type of work),
  2. Skill alignment (why your strengths fit the group’s workflow), and
  3. Bank-specific angle (why BofA’s franchise in this group stands out).

Example: M&A

  • Personal motivation: “I enjoy working on high-impact, complex situations where strategic reasoning and valuation intersect — M&A gives exposure to board-level decision-making.”
  • Skill alignment: “I like analytical modeling, synthesis of qualitative strategy and quantitative data, and working under pressure — all central to M&A.”
  • BofA-specific angle: “BofA has one of the largest M&A platforms globally, advising on cross-border and transformational transactions — such as [insert deal below].”

Example: Consumer & Retail (C&R)

  • Personal motivation: “Consumer trends fascinate me — brand loyalty, pricing power, and changing consumer behavior all directly impact valuation.”
  • Skill alignment: “I enjoy connecting qualitative brand narratives to quantitative performance — which is key in C&R.”
  • BofA-specific angle: “BofA’s strong relationships with companies like Mondelez, Coca-Cola, and Nike have kept it top-ranked in retail deal flow.”

Example: TMT

  • Personal motivation: “I’m drawn to how technology reshapes industries — from AI and semiconductors to streaming.”
  • Skill alignment: “I like learning fast and adapting — vital in TMT where business models evolve quickly.”
  • BofA-specific angle: “BofA has been a lead advisor on major telecom and software deals — like the Broadcom–VMware acquisition.”

2. Know Recent BofA Deals by Vertical

(Choose one per group and memorize the deal size, rationale, and BofA’s role.)

  • M&A: BofA advised Broadcom on its $69 billion acquisition of VMware (2023) — one of the largest tech deals in history, highlighting BofA’s M&A execution in strategic consolidation.
  • C&R: BofA advised Keurig Dr Pepper on its proposed €15.7 billion acquisition of JDE Peet’s (2024) — showing BofA’s leadership in cross-border consumer transactions.
  • TMT: BofA was lead adviser on Microsoft’s $68.7 billion acquisition of Activision Blizzard (2023) — underscoring expertise in large-cap tech and regulatory navigation.

(If any of these are too well known, you can swap in a smaller one from recent news — I can pull the latest specific deals for each group if you’d like.)

3. Identify 2–3 Industry Trends Per Group

Keep them current and business-relevant (macro + micro).

M&A

  • Consolidation after interest rate stabilization; deal rebound expected in 2025.
  • Strategic M&A to integrate AI, supply chain resilience, and efficiency.
  • Private equity reactivation as financing markets reopen.

Consumer & Retail

  • Shift toward value-conscious consumption and private labels as inflation persists.
  • Rise of omnichannel retail and direct-to-consumer strategies.
  • ESG and sustainability-driven rebranding (e.g., circular fashion, carbon labeling).

TMT

  • Acceleration of AI infrastructure investment (semiconductors, data centers, software).
  • Telecom consolidation and fiber expansion in developed markets.
  • Continued media convergence — streaming M&A, content differentiation battles.

4. Frame Questions to Ask the Interviewers

This shows engagement and initiative.

  • “How has deal activity in your group evolved over the past year given higher interest rates?”
  • “What differentiates the work-life balance and deal exposure between your group and others at BofA?”
  • “How do junior bankers add value during live deals in your group?”

5. Practice the Delivery

  • Keep each answer under 90 seconds and story-driven.
  • Be confident but conversational — treat it like a dialogue, not a script.
  • Have one numerical or concrete fact per answer (e.g., deal size, growth rate, trend stat).
  • If you’re asked “rank your preferences,” show clear rationale: “I’m most interested in C&R because of my consumer brand interest, but I’d be excited by M&A or TMT given their analytical and fast-paced environments.”

I have networking calls with people working in each of those three groups. Provide me with possible questions to ask them related to either the content of their work, industry, or more personal work life questions

Excellent — networking calls are where you demonstrate genuine curiosity and build relationships that can influence your group placement. You want to ask thoughtful questions that go beyond what’s on Google, balancing technical curiosity, industry awareness, and personal connection.

Below are targeted questions for each group (M&A, Consumer & Retail, and TMT), separated by three categories:

  1. Content / Nature of Work
  2. Industry & Market Trends
  3. Personal / Cultural Insight

💼 M&A (Mergers & Acquisitions)

1. Content of Work

  • “When you’re working on a live deal, how do analysts typically divide time between modeling, due diligence, and client materials?”
  • “What aspects of M&A execution tend to be most challenging or rewarding from your perspective?”
  • “How much client interaction do analysts get, especially on smaller or mid-market deals?”
  • “Can you walk me through what the internal process looks like after a company decides to explore strategic alternatives?”
  • “What kind of modeling or valuation work is most common in your group — LBOs, accretion/dilution, synergies, or all of the above?”

2. Industry / Market Trends

  • “Has deal flow started to pick up as rates stabilize? Are you seeing more strategic buyers or private equity interest?”
  • “What industries have been driving the bulk of your team’s M&A activity recently?”
  • “How is AI and data analytics influencing how clients think about acquisition targets or valuation?”
  • “Do you see a difference in how clients approach cross-border vs. domestic deals in the current environment?”

3. Personal / Work-Life

  • “What’s the steepest learning curve you faced when you joined the M&A group?”
  • “What makes the culture in BofA’s M&A team unique compared to other banks or groups?”
  • “How do you personally manage the intensity of deal timelines?”
  • “Was there a moment early on when you realized you were getting real responsibility?”

🛍️ Consumer & Retail (C&R)

1. Content of Work

  • “How does your team balance qualitative aspects like brand value and consumer loyalty with quantitative valuation?”
  • “How much time do analysts spend tracking company performance versus building materials for live deals?”
  • “Do you work more on sell-sides, buy-sides, or strategic advisory engagements?”
  • “How do you model brand-driven businesses differently from industrial or tech clients?”
  • “Are there differences in how you value global consumer companies vs. domestic ones?”

2. Industry / Market Trends

  • “What trends have had the biggest impact on consumer M&A over the past year — inflation, supply chains, or digital transformation?”
  • “How are clients adapting to shifting consumer preferences, like value-based spending or health/wellness?”
  • “Do you see more consolidation in certain subsectors, like food & beverage vs. apparel & luxury?”
  • “How do sustainability or ESG factors influence valuation and investor interest in consumer companies?”
  • “What differentiates BofA’s C&R franchise when pitching to clients versus competitors like JPM or GS?”

3. Personal / Work-Life

  • “What do you enjoy most about covering consumer brands versus other industries?”
  • “How does your team stay close to consumer trends — are you expected to follow earnings, retail data, or industry reports regularly?”
  • “What’s a memorable deal you’ve worked on that captures the essence of C&R banking?”
  • “How collaborative is your group across geographies — do you work with London or Asia consumer teams?”
  • “What’s the team culture like? Is it more analytical or relationship-driven?”

💻 TMT (Technology, Media & Telecom)

1. Content of Work

  • “What’s the balance between tech sub-sectors — software, semiconductors, media, telecom — in your coverage?”
  • “How do analysts stay up-to-date on rapidly evolving business models in tech?”
  • “How often do you work with product groups like LevFin or ECM for tech clients?”
  • “How do you approach valuation for high-growth or unprofitable tech firms?”
  • “Is there more focus on strategic M&A or capital markets in your group recently?”

2. Industry / Market Trends

  • “What emerging tech themes are driving deal activity — AI infrastructure, cloud migration, or semiconductors?”
  • “How have recent regulatory challenges (like antitrust scrutiny) affected tech M&A strategies?”
  • “Are clients still focused on scale acquisitions, or are tuck-in deals more common in this rate environment?”
  • “How are telecom and data-center deals changing with AI’s demand for infrastructure?”
  • “What differentiates BofA’s TMT franchise in terms of client relationships or sector expertise?”

3. Personal / Work-Life

  • “How would you describe the culture of the TMT group — more fast-paced and dynamic, or methodical and analytical?”
  • “What surprised you most when you started covering tech clients?”
  • “How do you stay motivated or curious in such a fast-changing space?”
  • “What kinds of people thrive in TMT at BofA — generalists, or people with niche tech interests?”
  • “Have you had opportunities to work on international tech transactions or cross-border teams?”

🎯 General Questions (For Any Group)

These are flexible “closing” questions that always leave a good impression:

  • “How do you see the group evolving over the next few years as the market shifts?”
  • “What separates top-performing analysts or interns from the rest in your experience?”
  • “What’s one misconception people often have about your group?”
  • “If you were choosing your group again today, would you still pick this one — and why?”
  • “What advice would you give to someone hoping to contribute meaningfully during their internship?”
